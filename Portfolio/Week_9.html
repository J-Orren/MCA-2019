Extracting Features

For this week's task, I extracted Spectrograms, Chromagrams and Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients from the same three tracks that I used last week ("Memories Renewed", "Annalise", and "Nocturno 2"). All of these can be found below.

Spectrograms show which frequencies are found within a piece of music.

Chromagrams show which pitch classes are found within a piece of music.

MFCC's show what kind of timbre or instrumentation is found within a piece of music.

Comparisons of Histograms

In comparing the MFCC histograms of all three tracks, there are clear differences between them all, which follows as no two tracks share the same genre or exact same instrumentation. That being said, the histograms for "Annalise" and "Nocturno 2" are the most similar to each other of all the tracks. This generally makes sense, as although they belong to quite different genres, the main instruments found in both are the human voice (although one is male and the other is female) and an acoustic guitar.

The histograms for "Melodies Renewed" are much more different to the other two tracks (see features 2, 3, 9, 14 and 20 for prime examples). Again, this makes sense, as most of the instrumentation in the track comes from piano, percussion, and some very synthy sound manipulation, not present in either of the other two tracks.
